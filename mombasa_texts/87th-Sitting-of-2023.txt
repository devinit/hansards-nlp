STATUS OF IMPLEMENTATION ON RESOLUTION ON INCLUSION OF KIOSKS TRADERS AS 
BENEFICIARIES OF MOMBASA  
 
The Deputy Speaker (Hon. Fadhili M akarani ): Mheshimiwa Samuel Mwaura?  
 
Hon. Mwaura:  Thank you Mr. Speaker.  Honourable  Speaker, I seek to give a response to 
statement which was issued by Hon ourable  Leila Nyache (MCA, Kipevu Ward), on the 
implementation of resolution on inclusion of Kiosk Traders as beneficiaries of Mombasa  
revolving fund loans.  Mr. Speaker Sir. The County Assembly initiated correspondence with the 
County Secretary as king to be furnished with the relevant answers pertaining to the above raised 
issue.  In his response, the County Secretary informed the committee that the Department of 
youth, Gender Sports and social services following consultations with the office of the Governor 
on Wednesday, 4th October, 2023 it was agreed that the department would propose a 
supplementary budget of one hundred million (100M illion ) for the revolving fund in the current 
financial year 2023/2024 to cater for Kiosk Traders, youth, women and vulnerable groups.  This 
means that Mr. Speaker the question of inclusion of Kiosk traders has well been catered for . 
Thank  you, Mr. Speaker .

IMPLEMENTATION STATUS OF THE MOTION ON AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER (ASD) IN THE 
COUNTY  
 
Mheshimiwa Samuel Mwaura?  
 
Hon. Mwaura:  Thank you Mr. Speaker. Mr. Speaker Sir, I would like  to address the 
statement issued by Hon ourable  Hamida Noor (Nominated MCA) regarding the Implementation 
Status of the Motion on Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) within our County.  Mr. Speaker  Sir, 
The County Assembly initiated communication with the County Secretary, seeking  pertinent 
information concerning the aforementioned matter. In response, the County Secretary informed 
the Committee that the County government, though the Department of Health Services, has been 
diligently executing the provisions of the Motion. Notably, the department has undertaken 
various awareness and sensitization initiatives aimed at educating our residents about autism 
spectrum disorder  (ASD).  On the 2nd of December, 2022, the Department of Health collaborated 
with Beyond Zero Medical Safari to organize a medical camp at Tudor Hospital for individuals 
with disabilities. During this event, childr en, along with their caregivers  received free diagnoses, 
caregiver awareness, psycho -education, and referrals for ongoing care.  A total of 115 children 
were assessed, with 32 of them diagnosed with autism and subsequently registered.  Furthermore, 
the County government observed World Autism Day on the 3rd of April, 2023, as part of the 
annual event dedicated to raising awareness and promoting inclusion for individuals with autism. 
The County Secretary reaffirmed the government’s commitment to sustaining these efforts and 
extending support to those affected by autism, underlining the presence of strong political will in 
this regard.  Thank you, Mr. S peaker Sir.
